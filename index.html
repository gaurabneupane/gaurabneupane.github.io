About

I'am Gaurab Neupane

I am a third-year PhD student in Mechanical Engineering specializing in thermo-fluid systems, computational modeling, and additive manufacturing, with a strong focus on plasma and wire-arc directed energy deposition (DED). My work integrates the development of computational simulation models (CFD and FEA) with experimental studies to understand and optimize complex multiphysics heat transfer and flow phenomena in additive manufacturing processes.

I grew up in Nepal, a country known for its rich culture, natural beauty, and the Himalayas, which inspired my curiosity and drive to explore new horizons. I earned my undergraduate degree from SRM Institute of Science and Technology in Chennai, India, and my master’s degree from the Indian Institute of Technology, Kharagpur. Outside of research, I enjoy singing, traveling, and playing sports.
